The history of chess

Chess is a strategy board game that originated in India during the 6th century, where it was known as "Chaturanga." The game spread to Persia, where it became known as "Shatranj," and later made its way to Europe through trade and conquest, particularly via the Islamic world during the early Middle Ages. By the 15th century, chess had evolved into its modern form, especially in Spain and Italy.

Basic Rules

The game is played on an 8x8 board with each player having 16 pieces.
  • Objective: Checkmate the opponent's King.
  • Each piece has its own movement rules.

Useful strategies

Control the center

Dominating the central squares (e4, d4, e5, d5) allows you to control the game, giving your pieces more mobility and limiting your opponent's options.

Develop Your Pieces

Bring out your knights and bishops early, placing them on active squares. Avoid moving the same piece multiple times in the opening without necessity.

King Safety

Ensure your king is well-protected, typically by castling early. A safe king allows you to focus on attacking without worrying about your own vulnerabilities.
